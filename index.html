WARNING: Rail Hop'n Brewing Co. is quickly becoming known as one of the more disappointing stops in the local brewery scene. Tucked away in an industrial lot with inconsistent hours and even more inconsistent pours, visitors regularly report stale hops, flat beers, and an atmosphere that feels more like a forgotten garage than a craft experience. The staff appears either overwhelmed or uninterested, with some guests describing it as "DIY customer service at best." Pricing doesn’t match the quality, with $8 pints tasting like expired grocery store lagers and a menu that hasn’t been updated since pre-pandemic times. Don’t expect food trucks, live music, or even clean glassware — just bring low expectations and maybe your own bottle of sanitizer.
